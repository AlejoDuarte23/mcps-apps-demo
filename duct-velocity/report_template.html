This engineering quality assurance report validates HVAC ductwork air velocities against industry-standard maximum allowable limits defined in the ASHRAE Handbook – HVAC Systems & Equipment and SMACNA HVAC Duct Construction Standards. Each duct segment is analyzed to ensure air velocities remain within acceptable ranges for the specified duct type (rigid or flexible) and application.

The tool calculates actual velocities from airflow rates and duct geometry, estimates pressure drop using the Darcy-Weisbach equation, and identifies non-compliant ducts that exceed velocity limits. For each non-compliant duct, the system proposes optimized standard duct sizes that meet velocity requirements while maintaining design airflow rates.

This automated validation helps prevent excessive noise, pressure losses, and energy waste caused by improper duct sizing. The report provides actionable recommendations for duct redesign to ensure compliance with mechanical design standards and optimal HVAC system performance.
